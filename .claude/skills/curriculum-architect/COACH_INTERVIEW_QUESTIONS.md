# Coach Interview Questions

Use these questions when interviewing swim coaches to gather methodology input for curriculum design. The goal is to identify **universal principles** (what all coaches agree on) vs **methodological preferences** (style differences).

## Interview Setup

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Format**: Video call or in-person (record with permission)
**Materials**: Share the draft curriculum beforehand if possible

**Opening script**:
> "I'm building a swim school curriculum for adult beginners. I've created a draft based on one coach's input, and I want to validate it with experienced coaches who may have different approaches. I'm not looking for you to agree with everything—I want to understand where coaches disagree and why. There are no wrong answers."

---

## Section 1: Background (5 minutes)

### 1.1 Experience
- How long have you been coaching swimming?
- What populations do you primarily teach? (kids, adults, competitive, recreational)
- What's your coaching certification/background?

### 1.2 Context
- Where do you typically teach? (pool type, indoor/outdoor)
- What's your typical class size for adult beginners?
- How long are your sessions usually?

---

## Section 2: Skill Progression (15 minutes)

### 2.1 Water Confidence Phase
- **When a complete beginner adult comes to you, what's the FIRST thing you teach?**
  - Follow-up: Why that specifically?

- **How do you approach students with water anxiety or past trauma?**
  - Follow-up: Any specific techniques that work well?

- **Do you start in shallow or deep water? Why?**
  - Note: Joseph uses "big pool first" for confidence building

- **How long does the water confidence phase typically take?**
  - Follow-up: What indicates a student is ready to move on?

### 2.2 Floating
- **When do you introduce back floating in your progression?**
  - Follow-up: Is it essential or optional for beginners?

- **Some students struggle with back floating more than front floating. How do you handle this?**

- **Do you consider floating a prerequisite for swimming, or a separate skill?**

### 2.3 Kicking
- **How many weeks/sessions do you typically spend on kick development?**

- **Do you integrate breathing with kicks from the start, or teach them separately?**
  - Follow-up: If integrated, what method do you use?
  - Note: Joseph uses "6-kick drill" (6 kicks face in, 6 kicks face out)

- **What equipment do you use for kick instruction?**
  - Follow-up: When do you remove the kickboard/noodle?

- **What distance milestones do you use for kicks?** (e.g., 10m, 25m, 50m)

### 2.4 Arm Pulls
- **How do you transition from kick-only to adding arms?**

- **Do you use single-arm drills, catch-up drill, or go straight to full stroke?**

- **When and how do you introduce side breathing?**
  - Follow-up: Bilateral from the start or one side first?

### 2.5 Full Stroke Coordination
- **At what point do students start swimming "for real" (full freestyle)?**

- **What's a reasonable final distance goal for a 12-week beginner program?**
  - Note: Current target is 50m continuous

---

## Section 3: Session Structure (10 minutes)

### 3.1 Timing
- **What session length works best for adult beginners?**
  - Options: 45 min, 60 min, 90 min, 120 min
  - Follow-up: Why that length?

- **How do you structure a typical session?**
  - Warm-up duration?
  - Instruction vs practice ratio?
  - Cool-down/supplementary skills?

### 3.2 Frequency
- **How often should adult beginners practice?**
  - Once a week? Twice? More?

- **What happens when students miss sessions?**
  - How do you handle catch-up?

### 3.3 Group vs Individual
- **What's the ideal group size for adult beginners?**
  - Maximum you'd accept?

- **How do you handle mixed-ability groups?**
  - (some students faster than others)

---

## Section 4: Equipment Philosophy (5 minutes)

### 4.1 Core Equipment
- **What equipment do you consider essential for beginner instruction?**

- **What's your philosophy on flotation aids (noodles, floaties)?**
  - Confidence builders or crutches?

### 4.2 Equipment Transitions
- **How do you decide when to remove support equipment?**
  - Student requests? Skill demonstration? Time-based?

- **Do you use pull buoys for beginners?**
  - If yes, when and why?

### 4.3 Fins/Paddles
- **Do you use fins for adult beginners?**
  - Benefits/risks?

- **What about hand paddles?**

---

## Section 5: Safety and Psychology (10 minutes)

### 5.1 Trauma Prevention
- **What's your biggest fear when teaching beginners?**

- **Have you ever had a student have a panic/trauma experience?**
  - What happened? How did you handle it?
  - What would you do differently?

- **What safety protocols do you always follow?**

### 5.2 Confidence Building
- **How do you know when a student is ready to swim without close supervision?**

- **What's the most common mistake new coaches make with anxious students?**

### 5.3 Recovery Skills
- **When do you teach recovery to standing?**
  - Do you consider it essential or assumed?

- **Do you teach treading water to beginners? When?**

---

## Section 6: Curriculum Review (10 minutes)

*If you shared the curriculum beforehand:*

### 6.1 Overall Impression
- **What's your first reaction to this curriculum?**

- **What would you definitely keep?**

- **What would you definitely change?**

### 6.2 Specific Concerns
- **Is there anything missing that you consider essential?**

- **Is there anything included that you wouldn't teach to beginners?**

- **Is the pacing realistic for 12 weeks with 1 session per week?**

### 6.3 Milestones
- **Do the milestones match how you'd assess progress?**

- **Are any milestones unrealistic for beginners?**

- **What milestones would you add or remove?**

---

## Section 7: Methodology Differences (5 minutes)

### 7.1 Philosophy
- **What's the ONE thing you believe about teaching swimming that other coaches might disagree with?**

- **Is there a "traditional" approach you've abandoned? Why?**

### 7.2 Adaptation
- **How would you adapt your approach for:**
  - A 25-year-old athletic person who just never learned
  - A 50-year-old who had a near-drowning as a child
  - A 35-year-old who can dog-paddle but wants proper technique

---

## Closing

- **Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?**

- **Would you be willing to review a revised curriculum after I incorporate feedback?**

- **Do you know other coaches I should talk to who might have different perspectives?**

## Key Disagreements to Resolve

After all interviews, identify:

1. **Universal agreements** (all coaches agree) → Build into curriculum as requirements
2. **Methodology preferences** (coaches differ but all approaches valid) → Make curriculum flexible
3. **Strong disagreements** (coaches contradict each other) → Research further or pick one approach with rationale

Document the final decisions and reasoning.
