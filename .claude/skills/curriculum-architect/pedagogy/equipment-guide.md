# Equipment Guide for Adult Swim Instruction

This document defines when to introduce, use, and remove equipment during adult swim instruction. Equipment should build confidence and enable learning—never become a crutch.

## Core Principle

**Equipment is a scaffold, not a crutch.**

Scaffolding: Temporary support that enables learning, removed when no longer needed.
Crutch: Permanent dependency that prevents independent swimming.

## Equipment by Phase

### Phase 1: Water Confidence (Weeks 1-2)

**Primary equipment**: Pool noodles

| Week | Equipment | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Noodle (optional) | Under arms for anxious students during wall work |
| 1 | No equipment | For gliding and recovery practice |
| 2 | Noodle (available) | Students can request if needed |

**Key rule**: Many students don't need noodles in Phase 1. Don't automatically hand one out—it signals "you might sink."

**When to provide noodle**:
- Student asks for it
- Visible anxiety despite verbal reassurance
- Student cannot release wall without panic

---

### Phase 2: Propulsion Foundation (Weeks 3-6)

**Primary equipment**: Kickboard, pool noodle

| Week | Equipment | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 3 | Kickboard | Arms extended, focus on kick |
| 3 | Noodle under hips | For students whose legs sink |
| 4 | Kickboard | Continue, add breathing integration |
| 4 | Noodle (reduce) | Start removing for students ready |
| 5 | Kickboard | Distance building |
| 5 | No noodle (goal) | Most students should be noodle-free |
| 6 | Kickboard (transition) | Start reducing kickboard reliance |

**The Double-Support Problem**:
Some students need BOTH noodle (under hips) AND kickboard (for arms) initially. This is fine. Remove noodle first, then transition kickboard.

**Noodle Removal Protocol**:
1. Student kicks 25m with noodle under hips
2. Try 10m without noodle: "Let's see how you do"
3. If successful: "You don't need it anymore"
4. If legs drop: "No problem, use it a bit longer"
5. Never remove suddenly or as test

---

### Phase 3: Arm Integration (Weeks 7-9)

**Primary equipment**: Kickboard (transitioning out), optional pull buoy

| Week | Equipment | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 7 | Kickboard | Single-arm drills (hold with one hand) |
| 7 | Pull buoy (optional) | Isolate arms for catch practice |
| 8 | Kickboard (reduce) | Catch-up drill may not need board |
| 8 | Hand paddles (optional) | Feel the catch, only if technique is good |
| 9 | No kickboard (goal) | Full freestyle without equipment |
| 9 | Pull buoy (drill only) | Occasional use for arm focus |

**Pull Buoy Rules**:
- Use between thighs, not ankles
- ONLY for students with solid kick already
- Never for entire session—max 10 minutes
- Remove when transitioning to full stroke

**Hand Paddle Warning**:
Hand paddles increase load on shoulders. Only use for students with:
- Good catch technique (high elbow)
- No shoulder pain
- Adequate strength

Never use to "build strength"—they're for technique feedback only.

---

### Phase 4: Endurance & Refinement (Weeks 10-12)

**Primary equipment**: Minimal

| Week | Equipment | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 10 | None (primary) | Full freestyle, build distance |
| 10 | Pull buoy (drills) | 10% of session for technique work |
| 11 | None | Video review, full swimming |
| 12 | None | Assessment—no equipment allowed |

**Goal**: Students swim 50m with NO equipment for graduation.

## Equipment Decision Matrix

Use this matrix when deciding whether a student needs equipment:

### "Should I give them a noodle?"

| Situation | Answer |
|-----------|--------|
| First time in deep water | Offer, don't insist |
| Student asks for it | Yes |
| Student is visibly anxious | Yes, temporary |
| Student's legs are sinking | Yes, under hips |
| Student can already float | No |
| Student completed Phase 1 without | No |

### "Should I give them a kickboard?"

| Situation | Answer |
|-----------|--------|
| Teaching flutter kick | Yes |
| Integrating breathing with kick | Yes |
| Single-arm pull drills | Yes (hold with one hand) |
| Full freestyle practice | No |
| Student can't maintain body position | Add noodle instead |

### "Should I remove their equipment?"

| Situation | Answer |
|-----------|--------|
| Student requests removal | Yes, with supervision |
| Student completes skill without relying on it | Yes, try without |
| Student looks comfortable | Ask, then try |
| Student is struggling WITH equipment | No, address technique first |
| Student becomes anxious without it | No, not ready |

## Equipment Removal Conversations

### Removing the Noodle
**Coach**: "You've been doing great with the noodle. I think you're ready to try without it. I'll be right here. Want to give it a shot?"

**If student hesitates**: "No pressure. We can try next time."

**If student agrees**: Stay within arm's reach. Celebrate success.

### Removing the Kickboard
**Coach**: "Your kick is really solid now. Let's try the catch-up drill without the board—your arms will do what the board was doing."

**Transition**: Use catch-up drill as the bridge between kickboard and free swimming.

### Pull Buoy Misuse
If students become dependent on pull buoy:
**Coach**: "The buoy is great for drills, but it's doing the work your kick should do. Let's practice keeping your hips up with your kick instead."

## Equipment Logistics (Lagos Context)

### Sharing Protocol
With 10 students and limited equipment:
- Students swim in pairs or small groups
- While 2 swim, others rest with equipment available
- Rotate every 2-3 minutes
- Coach manages equipment distribution

### Bringing Your Own
Students may bring:
- Their own goggles (recommended)
- Their own cap (optional)
- Personal noodle (acceptable)
- Personal fins (acceptable, short fins only)

Students should NOT bring:
- Inflatable armbands (false security)
- Life vests (not learning tool)
- Long fins (bad for technique)

### Pool Equipment
UNILAG pool has limited equipment. Bring your own:
- 3-4 kickboards minimum for group of 10
- 4-6 noodles
- 2 pull buoys

## Equipment Progression Summary

```
Week 1-2:  Noodle (optional) → No equipment (goal)
Week 3-4:  Kickboard + Noodle → Kickboard only
Week 5-6:  Kickboard → Reducing kickboard
Week 7-8:  Kickboard (single-arm) → Catch-up (no board)
Week 9-10: Pull buoy (drills only) → No equipment
Week 11-12: No equipment → Assessment (no equipment)
```

**The trend is clear**: Start with support, progressively remove, graduate with independence.

## Troubleshooting Equipment Issues

### "They won't let go of the kickboard"
- Psychological dependency, not physical need
- Try: "Hold it with just your fingertips"
- Try: "Let it float in front of you, don't grip"
- Eventually: "Push it ahead and swim to it"

### "Their legs sink without the noodle"
- Weak kick OR poor body position OR low body fat
- Try: Ankle flexibility exercises
- Try: "Look at the bottom, not forward"
- If persists: They may need more kick practice before removing

### "They swim better with the pull buoy"
- Their kick is likely not strong enough
- This is a sign to RETURN to kick work, not continue with buoy
- Buoy masks the problem; removing it reveals what needs work

### "They refuse all equipment"
- Pride or misconception that equipment is "cheating"
- Explain: "Equipment helps you focus on one thing at a time"
- Offer choice: "Try it once, then decide"
- If adamant: Respect autonomy, but note it may slow progress
