# Skill Progression Framework

This document defines the 4-phase progression for adult beginner swimmers. Each phase has specific skills, equipment, and milestones that must be achieved before advancing.

## Overview

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | End Goal |
|-------|-------|-------|----------|
| 1: Water Confidence | 1-2 | Trust and safety | Glide 5m, recover to standing |
| 2: Propulsion Foundation | 3-6 | Kick mechanics + breathing | Kick 50m with breathing |
| 3: Arm Integration | 7-9 | Pull technique + coordination | Swim 25m freestyle |
| 4: Endurance & Refinement | 10-12 | Distance + technique polish | Swim 50m continuous |

---

## Phase 1: Water Confidence (Weeks 1-2)

### Purpose
Build psychological safety and fundamental water skills. Students cannot learn technique if they're scared.

### Skills (in order)

1. **Pool Entry/Exit**
   - Safe entry using ladder or edge
   - Exit without assistance
   - Never jump in (until confident)

2. **Wall Holds and Releases**
   - Hold wall in deep water
   - Release briefly, return to wall
   - Build up to 3-second releases

3. **Face Submersion with Breathing**
   - Blow bubbles at surface
   - Submerge face, exhale through nose
   - Goal: 10 breath cycles without panic

4. **Bottom Tapping (Depth Understanding)**
   - While holding wall, tap bottom with toes
   - Understand actual depth vs perceived depth
   - Stand flat-footed on bottom (with wall support)

5. **Standing Without Wall**
   - Stand on bottom without holding wall
   - 3 seconds → 5 seconds → 10 seconds
   - Builds trust in ability to recover

6. **Front Float + Recovery**
   - Float face-down for 5 seconds
   - Recover to standing using tuck-press-rotate
   - This is THE critical safety skill

7. **Gliding**
   - Push off wall in streamline position
   - Glide 3m → 5m
   - Recovery to standing at end

### Equipment
- Pool noodles (for confidence, not flotation)
- Wall access in deep section

### Milestones
- [ ] Enters pool without assistance
- [ ] Submerges face for 10 breath cycles
- [ ] Floats face-down and recovers to standing
- [ ] Glides 5m from wall push-off

### Duration
Can often be completed in 1 session (90 min) for students without severe anxiety. May take 2 sessions for fearful students.

### Common Mistakes
- Spending too long on this phase for capable students
- Rushing this phase for anxious students
- Not teaching recovery to standing (safety critical)

## Phase 2: Propulsion Foundation (Weeks 3-6)

### Purpose
Develop the kick as the foundation of swimming AND integrate breathing. These are NOT separate skills.

### Skills (in order)

1. **Flutter Kick Mechanics (Dry)**
   - Demonstrate hip-driven movement
   - Show ankle relaxation
   - Contrast: knee kick vs hip kick

2. **Flutter Kick with Kickboard (Head Up)**
   - Kickboard held at arms' length
   - Head above water
   - Focus on kick mechanics only
   - Goal: 10m without stopping

3. **The 6-Kick Breathing Drill**
   - 6 kicks with head in water, 6 kicks with head up
   - Exhale while head is in (bubbles)
   - Inhale while head is up
   - This is the KEY breathing integration drill

4. **Progressive Breathing Reduction**
   - 6 in, 6 out → 8 in, 4 out → 8 in, 2 out
   - Conditions students for quick breathing
   - By Week 5: breath on demand

5. **Distance Building with Kick**
   - Week 3: 10m with breathing
   - Week 4: 15m with breathing
   - Week 5: 25m with breathing
   - Week 6: 50m with breathing (may need rest)

### Equipment
- Kickboard (required)
- Pool noodle under hips (for students who drop legs)
- Noodle + kickboard combo for struggling students

### Milestones
- [ ] Kicks 10m with proper hip-driven form
- [ ] Completes 6-kick breathing drill for full length
- [ ] Kicks 25m with breathing
- [ ] Kicks 50m (with or without rest break)

### Duration
Minimum 3 weeks. Do not rush. Kicks continue into Phase 3.

### Critical Notes
- **Kicks don't stop when pulls start**. This is the most common curriculum mistake.
- Breathing integration happens HERE, not in a separate "breathing week"
- Use noodle under hips if legs drop—don't let students get exhausted fighting buoyancy

## Phase 3: Arm Integration (Weeks 7-9)

### Purpose
Add arm pulls while maintaining the kick and breathing established in Phase 2.

### Skills (in order)

1. **Single-Arm Freestyle with Kickboard**
   - Hold kickboard with one hand
   - Pull with the other hand
   - Focus on high elbow catch
   - Goal: Feel the pull, not just move the arm

2. **Catch-Up Drill**
   - Both arms, but hands touch in front before next stroke
   - Allows focus on one arm at a time
   - Maintains kick throughout

3. **High-Five Partner Drill (Week 8+)**
   - Two students swim toward each other
   - High-five with pulling hand at surface
   - Forces proper timing and rotation

4. **Side Breathing Introduction**
   - Rotate body, don't lift head
   - Breathe in the "trough" created by head movement
   - Practice on both sides (bilateral)

5. **Full Stroke Coordination**
   - Continuous arm pulls with kick
   - Breathing every 3-5 strokes
   - Goal: 10m of coordinated freestyle

6. **Distance Building**
   - Week 7: 10m freestyle
   - Week 8: 15-20m freestyle
   - Week 9: 25m freestyle

### Equipment
- Kickboard (for single-arm drills)
- Pull buoy (optional, for isolating arms)
- Hand paddles (optional, for feel of catch)
- Noodle under hips (only if still needed)

### Equipment Transitions
- Remove noodle when student can maintain horizontal position
- Remove kickboard when catch-up drill is smooth
- Pull buoy used temporarily, not as crutch

### Milestones
- [ ] Completes single-arm drill with proper high elbow
- [ ] Swims 10m catch-up drill
- [ ] Demonstrates side breathing (not lifting head)
- [ ] Swims 25m freestyle with breathing

### Duration
3 weeks minimum. Some students need 4.

### Critical Notes
- **Don't introduce pull without continuing kick practice**
- Side breathing uses body ROTATION, not head LIFT
- If breathing falls apart, return to kickboard drills

---

## Phase 4: Endurance & Refinement (Weeks 10-12)

### Purpose
Build distance, refine technique, and prepare for graduation assessment.

### Skills (in order)

1. **Distance Building**
   - Week 10: 25m × 2 with rest
   - Week 11: 35m continuous
   - Week 12: 50m continuous

2. **Stroke Count Awareness**
   - Count strokes per length
   - Fewer strokes = more efficiency
   - Goal: Consistent count across lengths

3. **Video Feedback Session (Week 11)**
   - Record student swimming
   - Review together
   - Identify 1-2 specific improvements

4. **Back Float Introduction (Supplementary)**
   - 10 minutes at end of sessions
   - Not a primary focus
   - Safety skill for resting mid-swim

5. **Treading Water Introduction (Supplementary)**
   - 10 minutes at end of sessions
   - Egg-beater or scissor kick
   - Alternative rest position

6. **Final Assessment Preparation**
   - Practice 50m swim
   - Review all milestones
   - Address any gaps

### Equipment
- Minimal—students should swim without aids
- Pull buoy for technique drills only

### Milestones
- [ ] Swims 50m freestyle continuous
- [ ] Maintains consistent stroke count
- [ ] Demonstrates back float for 20 seconds
- [ ] Can tread water for 30 seconds

### Final Assessment Criteria

| Category | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Distance Completion | 30% | Completes 50m without stopping |
| Stroke Technique | 25% | High elbow, full extension, hip rotation |
| Breathing | 20% | Rhythmic, controlled, bilateral capable |
| Body Position | 15% | Horizontal, no dragging legs |
| Confidence | 10% | Calm demeanor, self-corrects errors |

**Pass threshold**: 70% overall

## Phase Transitions

### Moving from Phase 1 to Phase 2
**Requirements**:
- Can recover to standing from any position
- Can glide 5m without panic
- Demonstrates controlled breathing (face in water)

**Red flags that indicate not ready**:
- Still grabbing for wall when floating
- Holding breath instead of exhaling
- Panic response to water on face

### Moving from Phase 2 to Phase 3
**Requirements**:
- Kicks 25m with integrated breathing
- Breath-on-demand capability (not counting)
- Maintains horizontal body position

**Red flags**:
- Still counting kicks to breathe
- Legs dropping significantly
- Gasping for air at surface

### Moving from Phase 3 to Phase 4
**Requirements**:
- Swims 25m freestyle with breathing
- Side breathing (not head lift)
- Continuous kick during pull

**Red flags**:
- Stopping kick during arm pull
- Lifting head to breathe (causing hips to drop)
- Cannot complete 25m without stopping

---

## Regression Protocol

When a student regresses (common after missed sessions):

1. **Don't shame**: "Let's review the foundations—everyone does this"
2. **Diagnose**: Which skill broke down?
3. **Return to previous phase drills** for 10-15 minutes
4. **Rebuild**: Progress back through skills
5. **Document**: Note regression in student record for future reference
