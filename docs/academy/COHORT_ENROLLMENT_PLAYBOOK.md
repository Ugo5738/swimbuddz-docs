# Cohort Enrollment Playbook — Get 10 New Students in 30 Days

**Owner:** Daniel
**Goal:** 10 paid academy enrollments at ₦150k each = ₦1.5M revenue this enrollment cycle
**Timeline:** 30 days
**Last updated:** 2026-04-29

## The math

| Lever | Realistic conversion | Inputs needed |
|---|---|---|
| Warm personal network (DMs, calls) | 15-25% | 50-70 conversations |
| Corporate wellness package | 1 yes = 5-10 students | 5 corporate pitches |
| Current member referrals | 10-20% (referrer→referee) | 30 active member asks |
| Free assessment session conversion | 30-50% of attendees | 1 free event, 20 attendees |
| Paid social ads | 1-3% click-to-paid | Don't run yet |

**Read this:** Paid ads will not get you 10 students in 30 days. Your fastest path is **warm network + corporate package + free assessment event.** The math says: 50 personal conversations + 1 corporate pitch landing + 1 free assessment session is a realistic path to 10–15 enrollments.

## Track A — Warm personal network (highest probability)

You said you have contacts at Flutterwave, PiggyVest, GTBank, Fidelity, RiseVest. That's the gold. Working professionals at tech-aligned and finance companies are exactly the ₦15-20k/month segment.

### Step 1 — Build the list (Day 1, 60 minutes)

Open a spreadsheet. Columns: Name, Company, Relationship, Last contact, Status, Notes.

Fill in **30-50 names**: every contact at those 5 companies + extended network (other tech, consultancies, banks, startup founders). Don't filter for "swimming interest" — you don't know yet.

Tag each:
- **Hot** — already mentioned interest in fitness, wellness, or learning to swim
- **Warm** — likely to engage even cold
- **Cold** — needs reactivation

### Step 2 — Personal outreach (Days 2-7, 1 hour/day)

5-7 personalised messages per day. WhatsApp or DM, not email. **Not a broadcast.** Each one is bespoke.

**Template (adapt every time):**

> Hey [Name] — [personal opener: how are the kids / how's [project] going / saw your post about [thing]]
>
> Quick one — I'm running a 12-week beginner swim cohort starting [date]. It's specifically built for adults who never learned, or who learned badly and gave up. Coaches, structured curriculum, milestones, the whole thing.
>
> I thought of you because [specific reason: you mentioned wanting to learn / you've been stressed and this would help / your work schedule fits Saturday mornings / you mentioned [Spouse] wanted to learn].
>
> ₦150k for the 3 months. Next cohort starts [date], we have [X] spots left.
>
> If it's not for you — totally fine, no pressure — but if you know someone, I'd love an intro.

**Why this works:** Specific, personal, low-pressure, ends with a built-in referral ask if they say no.

**Track every message in the spreadsheet.** Status options: Sent, Replied, Booked call, Enrolled, Declined, Referred [name].

### Step 3 — Follow-up (Days 8-14, 30 min/day)

People are busy. Anyone who hasn't replied in 5 days gets one follow-up:

> Hey [Name] — bumping this up in case it got buried. No worries if it's not the right time, just didn't want it to slip!

Stop after 2 messages. If they don't reply, mark "no response" and move on.

### Step 4 — Booked call → enrollment (Days 7-30, ongoing)

Anyone who replies "tell me more" gets a 15-min call (Google Meet or phone).

**Call structure:**
1. **2 min** — what's their context? (never swam? scared of water? want fitness? family pressure?)
2. **5 min** — show how the cohort matches their context. Walk through the curriculum doc highlights. Show 1-2 transformation stories.
3. **3 min** — pricing + payment options (full ₦150k, or 2 installments of ₦80k with ₦10k deposit penalty)
4. **3 min** — book them now. "Want me to send the registration link?"
5. **2 min** — referral ask. "Who else in your circle do you think would benefit?"

**Don't pitch hard.** Working professionals can smell desperation. The cohort sells itself if you let it. Your job is to clear obstacles, not to close.

Your current cohort students and active members are warm. Their friends are warmer than your cold network.

>

### Execution

This is high-trust, low-cost. Even if it only lands 2-3 enrollments, it's pure margin and it strengthens member loyalty.

## Track D — Free assessment event (Day 14-21)

One Saturday between Day 14 and Day 21, run a **free 90-minute "Intro to Water" assessment session.**

### What it is

- 90 mins at a partner pool
- Open to non-members
- Coach takes attendees through 3 simple exercises that test current ability + comfort
- Each attendee leaves with a **personalised assessment** ("you'd start at week 2 of our beginner cohort, and here's what to expect")

### Why it works

- Low commitment (free, 90 mins)
- High intent signal (people who show up are seriously considering)
- The water itself is the salesperson — once they're in and not drowning, the fear breaks

### Promotion (Days 7-14)

- WhatsApp blast: "FREE 90-min adult swim assessment, Sat [date]. RSVP — limited to 20 spots."
- Instagram + TikTok: 1 post, 1 story, 1 reminder
- Personal asks: 5 of your warm contacts get a "I'm running this free thing, no commitment, want to come?" message
- Referral push includes "bring a friend to the free event"

### Conversion

Out of 20 attendees, 30-50% (6-10) convert to paid enrollment within 7 days if you follow up well.

**Follow-up sequence after the event:**
- **Day after**: thank-you WhatsApp with a 1-line summary of their assessment + next cohort start date
- **Day +3**: check-in: "How are you feeling about it? Any questions?"
- **Day +5**: enrollment link, payment options
- **Day +7**: "Last reminder — cohort fills [date]"

## What NOT to do in this 30 days

- **Don't run paid ads.** ROAS at this scale is unproven; you'll spend ₦200k and get 1 enrollment.
- **Don't do "general" social media posts asking for cohort enrollment.** Algorithm doesn't know your buyer; specific personal outreach does.
- **Don't lower the price reactively.** ₦150k is correct positioning for working professionals. Lowering signals desperation. Discounts via referral or corporate channels = strategic. Discounts via panic = brand damage.
- **Don't add new cohort categories** to chase enrollment ("intermediate cohort," "kids cohort," etc.). Stay focused — fill the one cohort first.
- **Don't promise outcomes you can't deliver** ("learn to swim in 30 days" — no, 12 weeks). Honesty converts better than hype.

## After this cycle

If 10+ enrollments land: document what worked. Which channel? Which message? Which corporate pitch? **Codify into the social media + sales playbooks.**

If <10 land: don't blame channels. Audit:
- Is the offer right? (₦150k vs. ability to pay)
- Is the timing right? (cohort start date — does it match working professionals' calendars?)
- Is the curriculum compelling? (the website + landing page have to do work)
- Is your pipeline pace right? (50 conversations is a lot; if you only had 15, you didn't input enough)

The math is unforgiving. 10 enrollments = ~50 conversations + 1 corporate yes + 1 free event. Hit those inputs first; outputs follow.
