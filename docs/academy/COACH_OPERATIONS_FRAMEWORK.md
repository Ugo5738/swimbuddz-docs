## 1. Coach Relationship Model

### 1.1 Engagement Structure

SwimBuddz engages coaches as **Independent Contractor Partners**, not employees.

**Why this model:**
- Flexibility for coaches and SwimBuddz
- Scales across pools, programs, and cities
- Protects the SwimBuddz brand
- Keeps costs predictable for both parties
- Avoids employment law complexity

**The agreement clearly establishes:**
- Coach is an independent contractor
- No employer-employee relationship exists
- Coach maintains autonomy over how they deliver sessions (within SwimBuddz standards)

### 1.2 Revenue-Share Model

SwimBuddz operates on a **revenue-share model** rather than fixed per-session payments.

**How it works:**
- Learners pay SwimBuddz for cohort enrollment
- Coach receives a percentage of collected revenue
- Percentage varies by coach grade and program category
- Payments settled per 4-week block

**Benefits:**
- Aligns coach incentives with learner outcomes
- Lower risk for SwimBuddz during ramp-up
- Motivates coach engagement and retention
- Transparent and predictable for all parties

### 1.3 Internal Block-Based Accounting

While learners pay upfront (full program fee), SwimBuddz operates internally on **4-week blocks**.

**Why this structure:**

| What Learners See | What SwimBuddz Runs Internally |
|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| One price, paid upfront | 4-week accounting blocks |
| Clear commitment | Coach paid per block |
| Cultural alignment (African context) | Progress assessed per block |
| Financial safety | Dropouts handled cleanly |

**Example:**
- 12-week program at ₦150,000
- Learner pays ₦150,000 upfront
- Internally tracked as 3 × 4-week blocks
- Coach paid 3 times (once per block)
- Progress evaluated each block

### 1.4 Cultural Context (Nigeria/Africa)

This framework is designed for African realities:

- **Payment culture:** Full upfront payment reduces dropouts, excuses, and non-payment
- **Learning culture:** Swimming is still seen as optional/leisure; commitment must be built in
- **Finance culture:** Installments often lead to missed payments and ghosting
- **Trust culture:** Who teaches matters; coach reputation affects enrollment

**Policy:** Installments are only permitted for:
- Corporate/institutional partnerships
- University programs
- Long programs (24+ weeks)
- Sponsored learners

## 3. Program Complexity Scoring System

Each program/cohort is scored on complexity dimensions specific to its category. This determines the coach grade required.

### 3.1 Scoring Methodology

1. **Identify the program category** (1 of 7)
2. **Score each dimension** (1-5 scale)
3. **Sum total score** (7-35 range)
4. **Map to coach grade** (Grade 1, 2, or 3)

### 3.2 Universal Grade Thresholds

| Total Score | Coach Grade | Description |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 7-14 | **Grade 1 – Foundational** | Entry-level programs, standard conditions, lower risk |
| 15-24 | **Grade 2 – Technical** | Moderate complexity, skill-intensive, some specialization |
| 25-35 | **Grade 3 – Advanced/Specialist** | High complexity, elevated risk, specialized expertise |

## 4. Coach Grades

### 4.1 Grade Overview

| Grade | Title | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Grade 1** | Foundational Coach | Entry-level programs, standard conditions, supervised initially |
| **Grade 2** | Technical Coach | Independent operation, moderate complexity, can mentor Grade 1 |
| **Grade 3** | Advanced/Specialist Coach | High complexity, specialized expertise, leadership eligible |

### 4.2 Grade Capabilities

| Capability | Grade 1 | Grade 2 | Grade 3 |
|------------|---------|---------|---------|
| Run low-complexity cohorts | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Run moderate-complexity cohorts | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Run high-complexity cohorts | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Operate independently | After probation | ✅ | ✅ |
| Mentor junior coaches | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Assess Grade 1→2 progression | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Input on curriculum | ❌ | Consultation | Active contribution |
| Leadership track eligible | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

### 4.3 Assignment Rules

- A **Grade 1** program can be run by Grade 1, 2, or 3 coaches
- A **Grade 2** program requires at least a Grade 2 coach
- A **Grade 3** program requires a Grade 3 coach only
- Coaches are paid at the **program's grade band**, not their personal grade
  - Exception: SwimBuddz may offer premium for overqualified coaches on strategic programs

## 6. Compensation Framework

### 6.1 Compensation Philosophy

**Core principles:**

1. **Coaches are paid per 4-week block** (not per session, not per hour)
2. **Pay is based on revenue share** (percentage of learner fees collected)
3. **Grade determines base percentage**
4. **Category and complexity can adjust within the band**
5. **Coaches do not negotiate individually** (system is transparent and standardized)

### 6.2 What SwimBuddz Retains

SwimBuddz's share (35-65%) covers:
- Platform and operations
- Marketing and member acquisition
- Insurance and liability buffer
- Admin and coordination
- Facility relationships
- Curriculum development
- Community building

### 6.3 Base Revenue Share by Grade

| Grade | Base Revenue Share | Description |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|
| Grade 1 | 35-42% | Entry-level programs, lower complexity |
| Grade 2 | 43-52% | Moderate complexity, skill-intensive |
| Grade 3 | 53-65% | High complexity, specialized expertise |

### 6.4 Category Adjustments

| Category | Adjustment | Rationale |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| Learn-to-Swim (Core) | Baseline (no adjustment) | Standard operations, predictable delivery |
| Special Populations | +3-5% | Higher skill requirement, liability, emotional labour |
| Institutional | -2-3% | SwimBuddz handles sales, logistics, contracts |
| Competitive & Elite | +2-5% | Specialized expertise, retention value |
| Certifications | +3-5% | Certification authority, external compliance |
| Specialized Disciplines | +2-5% | Niche expertise, equipment knowledge |
| Adjacent Services | Variable | Depends on service type and commercial model |

### 6.5 Adjusted Pay Bands by Category

| Category | Grade 1 | Grade 2 | Grade 3 |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Learn-to-Swim | 35-42% | 43-52% | 53-65% |
| Special Populations | 38-45% | 46-55% | 56-68% |
| Institutional | 33-40% | 41-50% | 51-62% |
| Competitive & Elite | 37-44% | 45-55% | 55-68% |
| Certifications | 38-45% | 46-55% | 56-68% |

### 6.6 Additional Modifiers

These can move a coach within their band (not outside it):

| Modifier | Adjustment | When It Applies |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Cohort size exceeds standard | +2-3% | More learners than typical for program type |
| Location difficulty | +1-3% | Remote pool, traffic challenges, limited facilities |
| Time slot difficulty | +1-2% | Early morning (<7am), late evening (>8pm), weekends |
| Continuity bonus | +1-2% | Same coach for 3+ consecutive cohort blocks |
| New program pilot | +2-3% | First time running a new program (curriculum development) |
| Multi-cohort coordination | +2-3% | Coach manages 2+ concurrent cohorts in same location |
| Mentorship role | +2-3% | Coach is supervising/training a Grade 1 coach |

## 7. Coach Progression Pathway

### 7.1 Progression Philosophy

1. **Progression is earned, not automatic** (time alone doesn't upgrade a coach)
2. **Each grade has clear requirements** (no ambiguity)
3. **Progression is category-specific** (Grade 2 in Learn-to-Swim ≠ Grade 2 in Specialized Disciplines)
4. **SwimBuddz controls progression** (coaches don't self-promote)
5. **Regression is possible** (inactive coaches or performance issues can result in downgrade)

### 7.2 Grade 1 → Grade 2 Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Minimum time at Grade 1 | 6 months active coaching |
| Minimum coaching hours | 100 hours logged |
| Cohorts completed | 4+ cohorts with satisfactory ratings |
| Learner feedback | Average rating ≥ 4.0/5.0 |
| Incident record | No safety incidents or serious complaints |
| Credential upgrade | Complete required Grade 2 credentials for category |
| Assessment | Pass Grade 2 practical assessment (observed session + debrief) |
| Recommendation | Endorsed by supervising Grade 2+ coach or SwimBuddz lead |

**Timeline:** Typical 6-12 months from starting as Grade 1

### 7.3 Grade 2 → Grade 3 Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Minimum time at Grade 2 | 12 months active coaching |
| Minimum coaching hours | 300 hours logged at Grade 2 |
| Cohorts completed | 8+ cohorts with satisfactory ratings |
| Learner feedback | Average rating ≥ 4.3/5.0 |
| Incident record | No safety incidents, exemplary conduct |
| Credential upgrade | Complete required Grade 3 credentials for category |
| Specialization | Demonstrated expertise in at least one area |
| Mentorship | Successfully mentored at least 1 Grade 1 coach |
| Assessment | Pass Grade 3 panel assessment (practical + interview + scenario review) |
| Endorsement | Written endorsement from 2 Grade 3 coaches or SwimBuddz leadership |

**Timeline:** Typical 18-36 months from starting as Grade 2

### 7.4 Progression Process

**Grade 1 → Grade 2:**
1. Coach expresses interest in progression
2. SwimBuddz reviews eligibility against requirements
3. If eligible, coach completes any missing credentials
4. Coach schedules practical assessment
5. Assessment conducted by Grade 3 coach or SwimBuddz lead
6. If passed, grade updated in system
7. Coach assigned to Grade 2-appropriate cohorts
8. Pay band adjusts automatically

**Grade 2 → Grade 3:**
1. Coach expresses interest or is nominated by SwimBuddz
2. SwimBuddz reviews eligibility against requirements
3. Coach completes any missing credentials
4. Coach submits portfolio (cohort history, feedback, development activities)
5. Panel assessment scheduled (2-3 assessors)
6. Assessment includes:
   - Observed session with challenging cohort
   - Scenario-based questions (safety, conflict, progression decisions)
   - Interview on coaching philosophy and SwimBuddz alignment
7. Panel decision (pass / defer with development plan / not ready)
8. If passed, grade updated in system
9. Coach assigned to Grade 3 cohorts and specialist programs

### 7.5 Cross-Category Progression

A coach graded in one category must separately qualify for another.

**Example:** Coach Amara is Grade 2 in Learn-to-Swim. She wants to coach Competitive & Elite programs.

| Requirement | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| Base qualification (Grade 2 Learn-to-Swim) | ✅ Met |
| Competitive swimming background | Needs verification |
| Competitive coaching certification | Needs to obtain |
| Technical knowledge training | Needs to complete |
| Coaching hours (competitive) | Starts at 0 |

She enters Competitive & Elite at **Grade 1** and progresses within that category separately.

**Accelerated pathway:** If she has external competitive coaching credentials and verified experience, she may enter at Grade 2 after assessment.

### 7.6 Maintaining Grade

Grades are not permanent. Coaches must maintain:

| Requirement | Frequency |
|-------------|-----------|
| Active coaching | Minimum 2 cohorts per year |
| Credential currency | First aid/CPR renewed as required |
| Feedback threshold | Maintain minimum rating for grade |
| Professional development | 8 hours per year |
| Code of conduct compliance | Continuous |

**Triggers for review:**
- Extended inactivity (6+ months)
- Feedback rating drops below threshold
- Safety incident
- Conduct complaint
- Credential expiration

**Possible outcomes:**
- Maintain grade (with improvement plan if needed)
- Downgrade to lower grade
- Suspension pending investigation
- Removal from SwimBuddz coaching roster

## 8. Operational Policies

### 8.1 Coach Agreement Essentials

Every coach must sign the SwimBuddz Coach Agreement which covers:

1. **Role & Scope** - What the coach can and cannot do
2. **Brand & Representation** - How the coach represents SwimBuddz
3. **Payment & Revenue Split** - Compensation terms
4. **Safety & Liability** - Responsibilities and risk allocation
5. **Exclusivity (Soft)** - Restrictions on poaching and competing
6. **Termination** - How either party can end the relationship
7. **Confidentiality** - Protection of SwimBuddz information
8. **Program Ownership** - All curriculum belongs to SwimBuddz

See: [COACH_AGREEMENT.md](./COACH_AGREEMENT.md)

### 8.2 Assistant/Trainee Coach Path

New coaches enter through a structured path:

1. **Application** → Background check, swimming assessment
2. **Orientation** → SwimBuddz values, safety protocols, systems training
3. **Shadowing** → Observe Grade 2+ coaches (minimum 20 hours)
4. **Assisted coaching** → Support sessions under supervision (minimum 30 hours)
5. **Assessment** → Practical evaluation for Grade 1 certification
6. **Independent coaching** → Assigned to Grade 1 cohorts

### 8.3 Multi-Coach Cohorts

Some cohorts require lead + assistant structure:

| Situation | Structure | Compensation Split |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Large cohort (15+ learners) | Lead + Assistant | 70/30 of coach share |
| High-risk program (open water) | Lead + Safety Assistant | 65/35 of coach share |
| Training cohort (new coach) | Senior + Trainee | 80/20 of coach share |

### 8.4 Performance & Quality Metrics

Coaches are evaluated on:

| Metric | Measurement | Threshold |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Learner feedback | Post-block surveys | ≥4.0/5.0 (Grade 1-2), ≥4.3/5.0 (Grade 3) |
| Milestone progression | % of learners meeting milestones | ≥70% |
| Attendance rate | Coach session attendance | ≥95% |
| Safety record | Incidents per 100 sessions | 0 serious incidents |
| Retention rate | Learners continuing to next block | ≥80% |

### 8.5 Conflict Resolution

| Issue | Resolution Path |
|-------|-----------------|
| Coach-learner disagreement on progression | SwimBuddz has final authority |
| Schedule conflicts | Coach notifies SwimBuddz 48+ hours in advance; backup assigned |
| Performance concerns | Documented feedback → Improvement plan → Review |
| Safety incident | Immediate suspension → Investigation → Outcome |

### 8.6 Non-Compete Scope

The soft exclusivity clause prevents coaches from:
- Poaching SwimBuddz members for private coaching
- Running competing programs using SwimBuddz materials
- Redirecting learners without disclosure

Coaches MAY:
- Teach at other organizations (non-competing)
- Take private clients (not SwimBuddz members)
- Build their own brand (separately from SwimBuddz)

Geographic and time boundaries are specified in the Coach Agreement.
