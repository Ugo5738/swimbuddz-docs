# SwimBuddz Pricing Strategy

*Last updated: April 2026*

## Core Principle: Every Tier Includes the One Below It

Don't stack fees. If someone has to pay community + club, the mental math becomes "I'm paying just to keep swimming after I already paid to learn." That feels like a trap. One price, one tier.

---

## Tier Structure

### Academy: N50,000/month (cohort-based)

- Duration depends on cohort (e.g., 12-week beginner cohort = N150,000)
- Includes **first month of club access** after graduation
- Frame it as: "Your academy membership keeps you swimming for 4 months — 3 months learning, 1 month practicing with your club group"
- The day after graduation, they already have a place to be next Saturday. No decision needed. No payment needed. Momentum carries.

### Club (includes community access)

- Quarterly: N42,500
- 6 months: N80,000
- 12 months: N150,000
- Kicks in after the free first month post-academy
- By then they've had 16 Saturdays in a row — paying to continue feels natural

Lead with the quarterly price. Only mention the longer commitments if someone asks about discounts — don't overwhelm new prospects with options. (And there is no standalone paid 1-month package — the only "1 month" in SwimBuddz is the free month that comes with academy graduation.)

### Community: N20,000/year

- Entry point for people who can already swim and just want access to the broader network, events, gear recommendations
- Also where club members land if they pause structured swimming but stay connected

---

## The Pipeline

```
Cold contact → Academy (N150k for 12 weeks, includes 1st month club) → Club (N42.5k/quarter) → Community (N20k/year if they pause)
```

Every tier includes the one below it. Nobody pays twice. Every transition has a bridge.

---

## The Critical Transition Moment

The transition from academy to club is where you'll lose people. Your cohort runs 12 weeks. Then 4-week gap. If during that gap you also ask them to pay again, most will say "let me take a break and think about it." They won't come back. The habit dies in that gap.

Including the first month of club in the academy fee solves this. They graduate on Saturday, and the next Saturday they're already part of a club group — no friction, no payment, no decision.

### The bridge does three things

- Removes payment friction at the most vulnerable moment
- Keeps the habit unbroken (16+ consecutive Saturdays)
- By the time the quarter payment hits, they're already embedded in the club community

---

## Founding Members: First Cohort Graduates

Consider making the first **quarter** of club free for your first 8 graduates, not just the first month. These people are your founding club members. They'll:

- Prove the model works
- Become your first peer leaders
- Recruit the next wave through word of mouth

That's worth more than N340,000 in collected fees.

---

## Prospective Member Communication

When someone asks "what does registering in SwimBuddz entail?":

> SwimBuddz has 3 groups:
>
> **Community** — This is the home base for everyone regardless of swimming level. You get access to our events, open swim meetups, and the broader SwimBuddz network. Members look out for each other — you'll always pick up something new from fellow swimmers and coaches even at community sessions. N20k/year.
>
> **Club** — For people who want to swim together consistently every week. Structured Saturday sessions, group challenges, and a crew that holds you accountable. N42.5k/quarter.
>
> **Academy** — Structured cohorts where you actually learn — beginner programs, stroke development, competition prep depending on the level. A 12-week program is N150k.
>
> [Tailor the recommendation to their situation]

### Key principles for the pitch:
1. **Lead with what they get, not what they pay** — describe the experience first, then the cost
2. **Show one price per tier** — no decision fatigue for a first conversation
3. **Position community as a choice**, not a consolation prize
4. **Connect to what they've seen** or experienced if possible
5. **Tailor the close** — recommend based on their situation without making lesser tiers feel lesser

---

## Why This Works

- **No fee stacking** — each tier is one clean price
- **Bridge periods** eliminate the gap where habits die
- **Downgrade path exists** — community catches people who pause, keeping them in the ecosystem
- **Upgrade path is natural** — community members see club energy at events and want in
- **Academy grads don't have to make a decision** — they're already swimming with their club group before the invoice arrives
