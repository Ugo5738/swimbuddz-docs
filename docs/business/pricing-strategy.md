# SwimBuddz Pricing Strategy

## Core Principle: Don't Stack Fees

Club includes community. Academy fee includes first month of club. Every tier includes the one below it. Nobody pays twice. Every transition has a bridge.

## The Pipeline

Cold contact → Academy (₦150k) → Club (₦42.5k/quarter) → Community (₦20k/year if they pause)

## Tier Breakdown

### Community — ₦20,000/year
- Entry point for people who can already swim
- Access to the broader SwimBuddz network, events, gear recommendations
- Where club members land if they pause structured swimming but stay connected

### Club — ₦42,500/quarter (includes community access)
- Structured Saturday sessions, group challenges, peer accountability
- Kicks in after the free first month post-academy graduation
- By then they've had 16+ Saturdays in a row — paying to continue feels natural

### Academy — ₦150,000 per cohort (includes first month of club)
- 12 weeks of coaching + 1 month free club access after graduation
- Frame it as: "Your academy membership keeps you swimming for 4 months — 3 months learning, 1 month practicing with your club group"
- The day after graduation, they already have a place to be next Saturday. No decision needed. No payment needed. Momentum carries.

## Why This Structure Works

### The Transition Problem
Your cohort runs 12 weeks. Then 4-week gap. If during that gap you also ask them to pay again, most will say "let me take a break and think about it." They won't come back. The habit dies in that gap.

### The Bridge Solution
The free first month of club after academy graduation means:
- No payment friction at the most vulnerable moment
- The habit continues unbroken (16+ consecutive Saturdays)
- By the time the quarter payment hits, they're already embedded in the club community

## Founding Members Strategy

For first cohort graduates: Consider making the first *quarter* of club free, not just the first month. These 8 people are your founding club members. They'll prove the model, become your first peer leaders, and recruit the next wave. That's worth more than ₦340,000 in collected fees.
