# SwimBuddz Community WhatsApp Playbook

*Last updated: April 2026*

## Purpose

The WhatsApp group is the heartbeat of the SwimBuddz community. It's where members connect between sessions, where prospective members get a feel for the culture, and where the community stays alive even when nobody's in the water. This playbook defines the weekly rhythm, content formats, and moderation approach.

---

## Group Setup

**Group name:** SwimBuddz Community
**Group description:** Lagos' swimming community. Sessions, events, conversations, and everything swimming. swimbuddz.com

**Who's in the group:**
- All active members (community, club, and academy)
- Prospective members who've shown interest (open admission — this is the top of the funnel)
- SwimBuddz team and coaches

**Admins:**
- Founder (always)
- Community manager / designated moderator
- Peer leaders (club group leaders) — post permissions, not full admin

---

## Weekly Rhythm

The group has two anchor posts per week that create predictable conversation peaks. Everything else is organic.

| Day | Content | Owner |
|-----|---------|-------|
| **Monday AM** | Member Spotlight of the Week | Community manager |
| **Wednesday** | Volunteer opportunity post (when available) | SwimBuddz team |
| **Thursday PM** | Question of the Week | Community manager |
| **Friday** | Session booking reminder (before 3pm deadline) | Automated or team |
| **Saturday** | Post-session energy — photos, results, celebrations | Peer leaders / members |
| **Sunday–Tuesday** | Organic conversation, pool tips, gear chat | Everyone |

### Monday: Member Spotlight (9:00 AM)

The spotlight is the signature community ritual. One member featured per week.

**Preparation (Thursday/Friday before):**
Message the featured member privately:
> "Hey [Name]! You're our SwimBuddz Spotlight Member next week 🏊 Can you answer these 5 quick questions and send me a photo of you (swimming or not, your choice)? I'll post it Monday morning."

Send them the 5 questions (see question bank below). Give them the weekend to respond.

**Post format:**

```
🏊 *SwimBuddz Member Spotlight*
_This week: [First Name]_

📸 [Photo]

*5 Quick Facts:*
1️⃣ [Question]: [Their answer]
2️⃣ [Question]: [Their answer]
3️⃣ [Question]: [Their answer]
4️⃣ [Question]: [Their answer]
5️⃣ [Question]: [Their answer]

💬 Drop a comment if you know [Name] or want to say hello! 👋
```

**After posting:**
- React to the post yourself with an emoji
- Tag 2-3 members who have a connection to the featured person: "@Temi you also swim at Federal Palace right?"
- Reply to every comment in the first hour to keep momentum going
- If conversation dies, ask a follow-up question about one of their answers

**Selection order:**
- Weeks 1-4: Pick your most outgoing, interesting members. They set the tone.
- Weeks 5+: Mix of established and newer members. Everyone should want to be featured.
- Always have 3-4 spotlights pre-collected so you never miss a Monday.

### Thursday: Question of the Week (2:00 PM)

A single question thrown to the entire group. No featured member — everyone answers.

**Post format:**

```
🤔 *Question of the Week*

[Question here]

Drop your answer below 👇
```

**After posting:**
- Answer the question yourself first (sets the tone, shows it's safe to share)
- React to every response with an emoji
- Create connections: "Oh interesting — @Chidi said something similar!"
- Pin the post so latecomers see it

## Spotlight Question Bank

Each spotlight uses 4 fixed questions + 1 rotating wildcard. This keeps them comparable while adding variety.

### Fixed Questions (always use these 4):
1. What do you do during the week?
2. How did you start swimming / what brought you to SwimBuddz?
3. What's your favourite pool in Lagos?
4. What's one thing swimming has changed for you?

### Wildcard Questions (rotate one per week):
1. What's your go-to post-swim food?
2. What song gets you hyped before a swim?
3. If you could swim anywhere in the world, where?
4. What's something people wouldn't guess about you?
5. What did Saturday mornings look like before SwimBuddz?
6. What's the best trip you've ever taken?
7. If SwimBuddz went on a group trip, where should we go?
8. What's a skill you'd love to learn next (outside of swimming)?
9. What's the funniest thing that's happened to you at a pool?
10. What would you tell someone who's scared to start swimming?
11. Morning swim or evening swim — and why?
12. What's your swimming goal for this year?

## Moderation Guidelines

### Tone
The group should feel like a friend's living room — welcoming, energetic, occasionally silly, never corporate. SwimBuddz team posts should sound like a person, not a brand.

### Rules (pin these in the group description)

```
SwimBuddz Community Rules:
1. Be kind. We're all here because we love swimming.
2. No spam, promotions, or forwarded broadcasts.
3. Keep it relevant — swimming, fitness, community, Lagos life.
4. Respect everyone's swimming level. No shaming.
5. Questions are encouraged. No stupid questions here.
6. Admin reserves the right to remove disruptive members.
```

### What to moderate:
- **Spam / promotions:** Remove immediately. One warning, then remove from group.
- **Off-topic chains that go too long:** Let them run for a bit (organic conversation is good), but if it drowns out community content, gently redirect: "Love this conversation! Let's get back to swimming — anyone have a pool recommendation for the mainland?"
- **Negativity / complaints:** Don't delete unless it's abusive. Acknowledge publicly ("That's fair feedback, thanks for sharing"), then take the conversation to DM if it needs resolution.
- **Silence:** If the group goes quiet for 2+ days, drop a casual prompt: "Who's swimming this weekend?" or share a swimming tip.

### What NOT to moderate:
- Off-topic conversations (within reason) — this is how relationships form
- Members recommending things to each other (restaurants, gear, etc.)
- Banter and jokes — if people are laughing, the community is alive
- Members organising informal swims outside of official sessions

### Handling prospective members:
- When someone new joins, welcome them personally: "Welcome [Name]! Great to have you here. Feel free to ask anything — we're all friendly."
- Don't hard-sell. Let them observe the community energy. The spotlight posts, question threads, and session recaps do the selling.
- If they ask about joining, give them the clear tier breakdown (see PRICING_STRATEGY.md) and point them to the website.

## Emergency Protocols

**If someone posts something inappropriate:**
1. Delete the message immediately
2. Send them a private message explaining why
3. If severe (harassment, abuse), remove from group without warning

**If there's a safety incident at a session:**
1. Post a brief, factual update to the group: "There was an incident at today's session. Everyone is safe. We'll share more details once we have them."
2. Do NOT let speculation run wild — pin the official update
3. Follow up with a detailed post once the situation is resolved

**If the group gets too noisy (50+ messages in an hour):**
1. Let it run if it's positive energy (post-event excitement, etc.)
2. If it's a heated debate, step in: "Great discussion! Let's cool it here and pick it up at the next session."
3. Consider creating a sub-group for specific topics if it becomes a pattern
