# Pool Ambassador Program

Everything you need to run the Pool Ambassador program — scripts, templates, orientation materials, and the prospects group playbook.

---

## A. Ambassador Poolside Script

This is a conversation guide, not a sales pitch. The goal is to be human, answer questions, and offer a low-friction next step.

### Step 1: Open (Warm, Casual)

Walk up naturally. Don't wait for them to approach you.

"Hey! I noticed you watching — are you interested in swimming, or just enjoying the view?"

If they seem shy or hesitant:
"No pressure at all — I just wanted to say hi. I'm [Name], I swim with this group."

### Step 2: Read the Situation

Listen more than you talk. Let them lead with their questions. Common ones:

- "What is this?" → "We're SwimBuddz — a swimming community here in Lagos. We swim together every week. Some of us are learning, some are experienced, we just enjoy it together."
- "Can anyone join?" → "Absolutely. We have complete beginners and people who've been swimming for years. Everyone's welcome."
- "How much does it cost?" → "Session fees vary depending on what you're joining. The easiest way to find out is to join our WhatsApp group — all the info is there."
- "I don't know how to swim" → "That's actually really common here. A lot of our members started exactly where you are. We have coaching classes for beginners too."

### Step 3: Gauge Interest

If they're asking questions, they're interested. Move to the close:

"Would you like to join our WhatsApp group? We share session info, photos, and you can ask any questions there. No commitment."

### Step 4: Collect & Add

- Take their phone number
- Add them to the "SwimBuddz — New Here?" WhatsApp group right there
- "I've just added you — you'll see a welcome message in there with everything you need to know."

### Step 5: Warm Goodbye

"Great meeting you! My name's [Name] by the way. Feel free to message in the group anytime. Hope to see you in the water soon."

### If They're Not Ready

Some people will just want to watch. That's fine.

"No worries at all. We're here on Saturday every week. If you ever want to know more, just come say hi."

Don't push. The seed is planted.

### Key Principles

- Be warm, not salesy
- Listen more than you talk
- The close is always the WhatsApp group — nothing more
- One conversation is enough. Don't follow up poolside if they said no
- Note how many people you spoke to (quick mental count, tell the Session Lead after)

## C. Ambassador Orientation Guide

Share this with anyone who takes up the Pool Ambassador role. Can be sent as a WhatsApp document or read in person before their first shift.

---

POOL AMBASSADOR ORIENTATION

Welcome to the Ambassador Role

Thanks for volunteering! As a Pool Ambassador, you're the bridge between SwimBuddz and the curious people at the pool who might become our next members. This isn't sales — it's just being friendly and helpful.

What You'll Do

- During the session, keep an eye out for non-members watching, asking questions, or lingering near our group
- Walk up and have a casual conversation
- If they're interested, add them to the "SwimBuddz — New Here?" WhatsApp group
- That's it. The group does the rest.

Before the Session

- Arrive with enough time to be briefed by the Session Lead
- Make sure you have the prospects WhatsApp group link saved on your phone
- Review the conversation script (Section A above) — you don't need to memorise it, just know the flow

During the Session

- You're not glued to the poolside. Swim, participate, enjoy the session
- Between your sets or during breaks, scan the area for onlookers
- Approach anyone who seems curious. If they're not interested, no problem — move on
- Aim to speak to at least 2-3 people per session (some sessions you might get 0 — that's fine too)

After the Session

- Tell the Session Lead how many people you spoke to and how many you added to the group
- Send a quick follow-up message to anyone you added (template below in Section F)

The Do's

- Be genuinely friendly — people can tell when you're being scripted
- Listen to their questions before jumping into explanations
- Make the WhatsApp group the only ask — never pressure someone to sign up or pay on the spot
- Respect people who aren't interested

The Don'ts

- Don't interrupt people who are clearly not interested
- Don't oversell — "we're a swimming community" is enough
- Don't make promises about pricing, schedules, or coaching you're not sure about. Say "the group has all that info" or "I'll find out for you"
- Don't collect money or promise free sessions

Reward
You earn 5 Bubbles per session as a Pool Ambassador. This is granted by the admin after the session is completed.

Questions?
Ask in the volunteer WhatsApp group or speak to the Session Lead.

## E. Re-engagement Message for Existing Prospects

Send this as a personal WhatsApp message (not in a group) to people who previously showed interest but went quiet:

---

For warm prospects (asked questions, showed real intent):

Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name] from SwimBuddz. We spoke a while back at [pool/event/context if you remember].

Just wanted to let you know we've been growing — we swim every week now and have sessions for all levels, including complete beginners.

I've added you to our info group in case you want to check it out: [link to "SwimBuddz — New Here?" group]

No pressure at all — just thought I'd reach out. Hope to see you at the pool sometime!

---

For lukewarm/cold prospects (expressed interest once, went quiet):

Hey [Name]! This is [Your Name] from SwimBuddz, the swimming community in Lagos.

We've got a lot going on these days — weekly sessions, coaching classes, and a growing community of swimmers.

If you're still curious, here's our info group where you can learn more and ask questions: [link]

Either way, hope you're doing well!

---

One message only. If no response, move on. Don't follow up again.

## G. Weekly Content Rhythm for Prospects Group

Keep the group alive without spamming. Maximum 2-3 posts per week.

| Day       | Post                                       | Example                                                                                                                    |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Monday    | Session recap photo/video from the weekend | A group photo or short clip with a caption like "Another great Saturday in the water. 22 swimmers this week!"              |
| Wednesday | A question, tip, or fun fact               | "Fun fact: swimming burns more calories than running and is easier on your joints. Who's joining us this weekend?"         |
| Friday    | Soft invite for the weekend session        | "We're swimming this Saturday at [location]. Beginners welcome. DM me or check [website link] to book your first session." |

Rules for the group:

- Never post more than once a day
- Keep it visual — photos and short videos perform best
- Don't hard-sell. The content should make people want to ask questions
- If someone asks a question, respond within a few hours (assign a group admin for this)
- Once someone signs up on the platform, move them to the main community groups and congratulate them in the prospects group: "[Name] just joined! Welcome to the community!" — this creates social proof for everyone else watching

## H. Conversion Tracking

Keep it simple. After each session, the ambassador reports to the Session Lead:

- People approached: [number]
- Added to prospects group: [number]
- Already knew about SwimBuddz: [number]

The Session Lead logs this in a simple note or spreadsheet. Over time, you'll see:

- How many conversations it takes to get one group add
- How many group adds it takes to get one signup
- Which sessions/locations generate the most interest

This data tells you where to deploy ambassadors and when.
