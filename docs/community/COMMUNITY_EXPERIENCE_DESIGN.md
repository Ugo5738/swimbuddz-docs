# SwimBuddz Community Experience Design

*Last updated: April 2026*

## Core Principle

**Community isn't a pool experience. It's a network.**

Most community members won't show up to swim regularly. That's fine — that's the point of why it's ₦20k/year and not ₦42.5k/quarter. The mistake is designing community around pool attendance and then feeling like it's failing when people don't come.

The community tier succeeds when people are talking, sharing, and staying connected to swimming in Lagos. Pool attendance is a bonus, not the metric.

---

## The Four Member Buckets

Every community member falls into one of these categories. Design for all four.

### 1. The Curious
They're interested in swimming but not ready to commit. They joined to stay close, learn, and maybe join the academy later.

**What they need:** Content and inspiration. Seeing others progress. Low-stakes ways to dip in.

**How to serve them:** Spotlight posts showing real member journeys. Pool tips. Academy cohort announcements. Monthly open meetups where they can show up without pressure.

**Conversion trigger:** They see club members having fun at the monthly meetup and ask "how do I join that?"

### 2. The Paused
Ex-club or ex-academy members who stopped but don't want to disappear. They took a break — job changed, schedule shifted, had a baby — but still identify as swimmers.

**What they need:** To feel welcome back anytime without re-explaining themselves. No guilt, no pressure.

**How to serve them:** Keep them in the group. Don't single them out when they go quiet. When they re-engage, make it easy: "Great to see you — the club is swimming every Saturday if you want to jump back in."

**Conversion trigger:** Life stabilises, they remember how much they enjoyed it. The group kept the door open.

### 3. The Social
They don't care much about improving their swimming. They like the people, the energy, the community identity.

**What they need:** Social touchpoints. Events that aren't about swimming technique. A reason to show up and see people.

**How to serve them:** Quarterly social events (beach day, hangout, potluck). The WhatsApp group conversations. Post-swim meetups at a nearby spot.

**Conversion trigger:** They bring a friend to an event. That friend joins. They feel proud of contributing to the community's growth.

### 4. The Already-Swimmers
They swim independently — at their own pool, on their own schedule. They don't need instruction or a group. They want to be part of a swimming identity in Lagos.

**What they need:** Belonging signals. To be seen as part of something. Access to the network — pool recommendations, gear tips, swimming conversations.

**How to serve them:** The WhatsApp group is their primary value. The SwimBuddz identity (profile, membership card, being able to say "I'm a SwimBuddz member") matters to them. Invite them to the monthly open meetup.

**Conversion trigger:** They want structured improvement or to be part of a consistent training group. They know exactly where to go.

## What Community Membership Actually Looks Like

### Weekly (WhatsApp — this is 80% of the value)

| Day | What happens |
|-----|-------------|
| **Monday** | Member Spotlight drops. Conversation about the featured member. |
| **Wednesday** | Coach tip or open question: "What's one thing you struggle with in freestyle?" Gets people talking. |
| **Thursday** | Question of the Week. Everyone answers. |
| **Friday** | "Who's swimming this weekend?" — open invitation, no pressure. Members self-organise informal swims. |
| **Saturday** | Post-session energy from club members — photos, celebrations, personal bests. Community members see the club life. |

The Friday post is deceptively powerful. It requires no planning — just a message. But over time, members start responding: "I'm going to Federal Palace Saturday morning if anyone wants to join." That's organic community forming without any orchestration.

### Monthly

**Open Swim Meetup**
One Saturday per month, all tiers swim together at a rotating pool location. No structure, no coaching, no sign-in tracking.

This is the community's most important event because:
- It's the one time community members see the full SwimBuddz energy
- The Curious bucket watches club members get celebrated and decides they want in
- It functions as a showcase, not a service
- Community members can bring guests (lowest-friction acquisition)

The goal of the meetup isn't attendance numbers — it's conversion. Track how many community members sign up for academy or club in the two weeks following each meetup.

### Quarterly

**Social Event (non-swimming)**
Beach outing, rooftop hangout, movie night, potluck. This exists primarily for The Social bucket but deepens bonds across all tiers. No swimming required. Everyone's welcome.

**Bring a Friend Open Day**
Community members invite someone who's never swum with SwimBuddz. The friend gets a free taste of the community. Lower barrier than an academy cohort, higher exposure than a WhatsApp post.

## The Key Metric

**Community health is measured by group chat activity, not pool attendance.**

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Members posting unprompted | Community is alive |
| High response rate to questions | Members feel safe to engage |
| Members tagging each other | Relationships forming |
| Members organising informal swims | Community is self-sustaining |
| Group goes quiet for 3+ days | Intervention needed |
| Members only reply to official posts | Community is passive — needs more organic prompts |

When the group goes quiet, don't post more announcements. Post a question, start a conversation, or ask someone privately to share something. Announcements don't revive a quiet group — conversations do.

## How to Pitch the Community Tier

Don't say: *"You can join the community and learn from fellow members."*

That sounds like a consolation prize.

Say: *"You'll be in our WhatsApp group with [X]+ swimmers across Lagos. People share pool recommendations, organise informal swims, and we do a group meetup once a month. It's the easiest way to stay connected to swimming without a fixed schedule."*

That makes ₦20k/year feel like a bargain for access to a network.

## What the Platform Needs for Community Members

Don't over-engineer this. The community tier lives on WhatsApp. The platform is a supporting tool.

**What to build (now):**
- Profile that shows community membership status
- Events calendar with upcoming open meetups and socials
- RSVP for open swim meetups
- Attendance history (when they joined, events attended)
- Clear upgrade path to club or academy

**What NOT to build (yet):**
- Social feeds or forums in the app
- In-app messaging
- Community challenges or leaderboards (that's club territory)

The platform serves club and academy. Community members show up in the platform when they're ready to upgrade. Until then, WhatsApp is the product.

## Community → Club Conversion Flow

The most natural conversion path:

1. Community member joins WhatsApp group
2. They see club members celebrating personal bests every Saturday
3. They attend an open meetup and swim alongside a club group
4. They ask "how do I join the club?" — peer leader directs them to the founder
5. Founder has a 5-minute conversation, explains the tier, they sign up

The open meetup is where this happens most reliably. Make sure club members bring energy on those Saturdays — the contrast between "casual community swim" and "club group celebrating each other" is what sells the upgrade.

---

## Community → Academy Conversion Flow

1. Community member sees academy cohort announcement in the group
2. They see the progress report images that academy members post on Instagram/WhatsApp
3. They ask about the program — someone in the group explains it
4. They attend an open meetup and meet academy alumni
5. They enquire about the next cohort

Academy members posting their progress reports publicly is the most powerful community-to-academy conversion tool available. Encourage it actively.
