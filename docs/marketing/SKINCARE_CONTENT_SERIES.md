| 1 | **What to do BEFORE you swim** ⏰ | Most people skip this. It costs you skin. |

## Series 4 — "On Discoloration & The Swim Trunk Line" (Education)

### Asset 1: Instagram carousel (6 slides)

| Slide | Headline | Body |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The 'swim trunk line' isn't a tan.** | It's post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH). And it's preventable. |
| 2 | **What is PIH?** | A healing response. Any inflammation, friction, or irritation triggers melanocytes to overproduce melanin. |
| 3 | **Why darker skin is more affected** | Melanin-rich skin has more active melanocytes. The same trigger produces more pigment. |
| 4 | **What makes it worse** | Sun exposure compounds it dramatically — UVA, UVB, infrared all worsen pigment damage. |
| 5 | **How to minimize it** | • Sunscreen, broad-spectrum, reapply every 2-3h<br>• Swim early morning or evening<br>• Avoid 10am-4pm sun<br>• Swim in shaded pools when possible<br>• Stay consistent with the post-swim routine |
| 6 | **Recovery is measured in weeks, not days.** | Be patient. Be consistent.<br><br>*Dr. Adaeze, @sankora_dermatology* |

**Caption:**

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't film new dermatologist content yet — exhaust this asset first. 50 mins of recorded expertise = 12+ posts of value.
- ❌ Don't translate the doctor's exact words into casual language. Some specificity is the credibility ("post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation," not "dark spots"). Keep the medical-precise tone in body copy; keep the headline and CTA approachable.
- ❌ Don't over-claim. Add the disclaimer ("general education, not individual diagnosis") to the PDF and at least one carousel.
- ❌ Don't lose the partnership. Tag Dr. Adaeze + Sankora in every single post.
