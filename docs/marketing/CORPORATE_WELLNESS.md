# Part 1 — The Pitch (outward-facing)

## SwimBuddz Corporate Wellness

**The problem most wellness budgets don't solve.**

Your professionals work 50+ hours a week. Sedentary, screen-heavy, stressed. Wellness budgets get spent on gym memberships nobody uses past March, mental health apps that go unopened, and team yoga classes attended by the same five people.

Adults across Lagos share one quiet anxiety: **most of them never learned to swim**. Some are afraid of water. Some learned badly as kids and gave up. Most have never been given a structured way to fix it as adults.

**The SwimBuddz 12-Week Adult Swim Program** is an alternative wellness benefit that gets used, finished, and remembered.

### What employees get

- 12-week structured swim cohort, beginner-friendly
- Saturday mornings, 90-minute sessions, Lagos partner pool
- Trauma-informed coaching — designed for adults who never learned (most of your workforce)
- Distance-based milestones, structured curriculum, community of peers
- WhatsApp group, milestone certificates, post-program access to SwimBuddz Club
- A skill that lasts a lifetime — and doesn't require daily discipline

### Why swimming, specifically

- **Zero impact** — lower injury rate than gym, accessible to all body types
- **Real recovery** — water is regulating; cortisol drops measurably during swim
- **Lifelong skill** — once learned, never forgotten (unlike a new gym routine)
- **Community-driven** — group format, not solo gym time. Builds bonds your team retreats can't.
- **Photogenic outcomes** — your CSR / employer brand team will thank you

# Part 2 — Internal Playbook (don't share externally)

## Target buyer profile

**Who buys:**
- Head of People / HR Director (final say on wellness spend)
- Wellness / L&D Manager (recommends and implements)
- Office Manager (owns logistics, sometimes the final yes)

**Companies that fit (priority order):**

1. **Tech-aligned** — Flutterwave, Paystack, Andela, Carbon, Bamboo, RiseVest, PiggyVest
   - Rationale: young workforce, wellness budgets, photogenic culture, lower price-sensitivity
2. **Banks & finance** — GTBank, Access, Fidelity, Zenith
   - Rationale: large workforce, established wellness programs, conservative on novelty so frame as "evidence-based, structured, professional"
3. **Consultancies** — Big 4, KPMG, McKinsey Lagos
   - Rationale: stressed teams, recovery framing lands, generous benefits
4. **Telcos** — MTN, Airtel
   - Rationale: huge workforce, slow sales cycle but big deals when they land
5. **MDAs / parastatals** — Lagos State, federal agencies
   - Rationale: huge potential, slow procurement, only after 3+ corporate references

## Sales cycle (realistic)

- **First contact → first reply:** 3-7 days
- **First reply → intro call:** 7-14 days
- **Intro call → procurement decision:** 4-8 weeks
- **Decision → enrollment:** 2-3 weeks (HR onboarding, internal comms)

**Total: 9-13 weeks from first email to first cohort start.** Plan accordingly.

## Intro call structure (20 mins)

| Time | Section | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 min | Their context — what wellness programs do they run today? What's working / failing? | Understand actual pain |
| 2-7 min | SwimBuddz overview — curriculum, coaches, milestones, schedule | Establish credibility |
| 7-12 min | How it would work for [Company] specifically — pool nearest their HQ, schedule fit, pricing tier | Show fit |
| 12-17 min | Outcomes & reporting — what they'd see after 6 weeks and 12 weeks | Show measurability |
| 17-20 min | Next steps — pilot offer, internal champion, decision timeline | Move forward |

**Don't pitch hard.** HR buyers are evaluated on quality of choices, not enthusiasm. Calm, evidence-based, low-pressure converts. Aggressive doesn't.

## Pricing & negotiation rules

- **Hold the line at ₦150k retail.** Discounts only as documented bulk tiers (10% at 5+, 15% at 10+).
- **Pilot offer is the lever** — first 5 corporate partners get added perks (no setup fees, free intro session) instead of price drops.
- **Never go below ₦127.5k** even at 20+ employees. Below that, coach economics break.
- **Payment terms:** prefer 50% deposit / 50% at week 6. Don't accept net-90 from any company without an established commercial relationship.
- **Include in writing:** start date, employee names, pool location, refund policy (no refund after week 2; partial refund week 1).

## Internal handoff after a sale

When a corporate yes lands:
1. Cohort capacity check — does the upcoming cohort have room? If not, schedule a dedicated cohort.
2. HR contact gets a 1-pager: how employees register, what they need to bring to first session, WhatsApp group invite.
3. Each employee gets a SwimBuddz account (members service) with `acquisition_source = CORPORATE` (when that field ships).
4. Coach assignment + pool allocation confirmed at least 2 weeks before start.
5. Mid-cohort check-in with HR contact (week 6).
6. End-of-cohort report sent to HR contact (week 12 + 1).

## Outcome tracking (per corporate cohort)

- Attendance rate per employee
- Milestone completion rate
- Pre/post wellness survey (5 questions, ~2 min)
- NPS at week 12
- Photo/video consent + assets for internal comms

Aggregate into a 1-page report. **This is the single most important asset for your second sale to that company** — and for the next 4 corporate sales after them.

## When to walk away

- Buyer wants <₦100k/employee. Walk.
- Buyer wants weekday-only. Logistically incompatible — walk.
- Buyer wants to brand the program as theirs (white-label). Defer until you've done 5+ corporate cohorts; not the right move at this scale.
- Buyer wants to pay only on outcomes. Risky for a service business; politely decline.

## Tracking & pipeline

Maintain a corporate pipeline spreadsheet (or use a CRM). Columns: Company, Contact, Stage, Last touch, Next action, Deal size, Probability, Notes.

**Quarterly review:** which companies advanced? Which stalled? What patterns?
