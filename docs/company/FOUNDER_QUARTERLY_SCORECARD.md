# Founder Quarterly Scorecard

**Owner:** Daniel
**Cadence:** Reviewed quarterly (last week of each quarter)
**Last updated:** 2026-04-29

This is the founder's self-assessment instrument. It captures the 10 ultra-wealth-builder traits, 5 outcome metrics, and 3 reflection prompts. The point isn't the score in any single quarter — it's the **trend** over 8-12 quarters. Wealth compounds slowly and visibly when measured.

## How to use

- Fill out at the **end** of each quarter (last week)
- Write the score (1-10) **and one line of evidence** — a score without evidence is fiction
- Compare to previous quarters — direction matters more than absolute number
- Identify **one bet to make and one bet to kill** for next quarter
- Identify **one person to know** by next quarter
- File completed quarters in this same doc, keeping the most recent at the top

## Trait definitions (quick reference)

When in doubt about how to score yourself, return to these:

1. **Leverage creation** — How much of what you built this quarter works without your direct hour-for-hour input? Code, content, capital, brand, IP, relationships.
2. **Opportunity spotting** — How many real gaps did you see this quarter that others didn't? Did you act on them?
3. **Speed of decision** — How long did your hardest decisions take? Did you decide at 60-70% confidence, or did you wait for 90%? Reversible decisions don't need 90%.
4. **Long-term thinking** — Did you make decisions whose payoff is >2 years out? Did you say no to short-term wins that would damage long-term compounding?
5. **Capital allocation** — Was every ₦ and every hour deployed where it had the highest expected return? Where did you spend that you shouldn't have? Where did you not spend that you should have?
6. **Network quality** — How many new high-value relationships did you make? How many existing relationships did you deepen? Did you spend time with people 1-2 levels above your current scale?
7. **Emotional detachment** — When something failed this quarter, how fast did you recover? How fast did you act on data that contradicted your intuition? When you got bad feedback, did you ruminate or learn?
8. **Asymmetric bets** — Did you make at least one bet this quarter where the downside was capped and the upside was uncapped? Conservative steady-state work gets a 5; one real asymmetric bet gets a 7+.
9. **Ownership over income** — What % of your work this quarter built things you own (equity, IP, brand, network) vs. things that paid you for hours (services, consulting)?
10. **First-principles thinking** — Did you make any decisions this quarter by reasoning from physics, not from analogy? "Because everyone else does it this way" is the opposite of this trait.

## How the trend looks (fill in after 4+ quarters)

After Q4, start a simple trend chart at the top of this doc:

```
Quarter:    Q1   Q2   Q3   Q4   Q5   Q6   Q7   Q8
Composite:  __   __   __   __   __   __   __   __
Owner-h%:   __   __   __   __   __   __   __   __
RecRev (₦): __   __   __   __   __   __   __   __
```

**The wealth-building signal isn't any single number — it's whether all three of those rows are climbing over 8 quarters.** If the composite stalls but recurring revenue and owner-hour share keep rising, you're winning. If the composite climbs but recurring revenue is flat across 4 quarters, the score is lying.
