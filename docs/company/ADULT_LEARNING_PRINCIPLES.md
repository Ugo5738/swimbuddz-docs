# Swimbuddz Adult Learning Principles (Andragogy)

This document serves as the "Knowledge Base" for the **Curriculum Architect**. It defines *how* Swimbuddz teaches swimming to ADULTS.

## 1. Core Philosophy: Respect the Learner
*   **Fear is Real**: Many adult beginners have trauma or deep-seated fear. We validate this, we don't dismiss it.
*   **Why > What**: Adults need to know *why* they are doing a drill. "Because I said so" doesn't work. Explain the physics (hydrodynamics) and the physiology.
*   **Autonomy**: Adults want agency. Give them choices in the lesson (e.g., "Do you want to try that with fins or without?").

## 2. The Feedback Loop
*   **Technical but Accessible**: Use proper anatomical terms but explain them simply.
*   **Proprioception Focus**: Adults often struggle to know where their body is in space. Use video feedback and "feel" cues (e.g., "Imagine reaching over a barrel").

## 3. Adult Learner Segments
*   **The "Fearful Beginner"**: Goal is comfort, not distance. Focus on breath control, buoyancy, and standing up safely.
*   **The "Fitness Swimmer"**: Goal is calorie burn and health. Focus on continuous movement and efficiency.
*   **The "Triathlete/Pro"**: Goal is speed and open water skills. Focus on sighting, drafting, and stroke rate.

## 4. Assessment Standards
*   **Efficiency over Speed**: We grade based on "Ease of movement" not just the clock.
## 5. Beyond the Lesson (The Trinity Model)
*   **Community Integration**: Learning happens socially. Encourage students to attend Community events even before they graduate.
*   **The Club Goal**: Every student should know *why* they are learning: to join the Club. Use "Club Readiness" as a motivation tool (e.g., "This drill will help you handle the 1km Club Ocean Swim").
*   **Global Mindset**: We are building global swimmers. Teach universal etiquette/signals that work in any pool in the world.
