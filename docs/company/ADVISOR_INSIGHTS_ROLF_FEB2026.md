# Advisor Insights — Rolf Groenewold, February 2026

> Strategic insights from three conversations with Rolf (advisor, VC background, 10+ years in business valuation) on SwimBuddz's product-market fit, monetization, and positioning. Extracted and mapped to current implementation work.

---

## The Three Big Insights

### 1. North Star Metric: Hours in the Pool

> "If I was to define your North Star in this business, it's hours in the water. That's literally how I'd measure the entire business."

**What it means:**
- Every feature, every metric, every decision should ladder up to: **are people spending more hours in the pool?**
- Two levers: **more swimmers** (acquisition) × **more hours per swimmer** (retention/consistency)
- This metric captures everything — community engagement, session quality, coaching effectiveness, volunteer support

**How it maps to the homepage:**
- The homepage's job is **acquisition** — getting someone to register. But the *messaging* should promise **consistency** (hours in pool), not just community.
- The "How It Works" section Step 3 ("Show Up Consistently") is already on-point — it directly speaks to hours in pool
- The Bubbles rewards system is a retention mechanism — it encourages more hours
- Volunteer system keeps the experience good → people come back → more hours

### 2. Swimming Pools as Distribution Partners

> "I would focus on the swimming pools as my distribution channel... You should pay me, as SwimBuddz, for your right to have SwimBuddz as part of your pool."

**The argument to pools:**
- SwimBuddz increases swimmer frequency (more visits = more pool revenue)
- Amazon Prime analogy: Prime members spend 8x more than non-Prime. SwimBuddz members will visit the pool 3-5x more than casual visitors.
- SwimBuddz keeps swimming "front of mind" in a world of increasing distractions
- Community and accountability drive consistency
- Pools should *pay* for SwimBuddz distribution because it directly increases their revenue

**How it maps to current work:**
- This is a **future B2B play** — not homepage relevant right now, but it's critical context for the overall strategy
- The "Where We Swim" section on the About page already lists pool locations — this could evolve into a partnership showcase
- The North Star metric (hours in pool) is exactly the metric that aligns SwimBuddz with pool operator incentives

### 3. Emotion-Led Messaging — Speak to Fear

> "Speak to the problem you're trying to solve. Articulate how people actually feel. When somebody reads language that is actually how they feel, then they feel like you understand them."
>
> "Learn, train and enjoy swimming with the swimming community — it's like, yeah, you can't go wrong with that. It's very middle of the road."

**What it means:**
- The current website copy is professional but doesn't speak to the **emotional reality** of the target audience
- Target audience = people who are **scared of water**, live in areas where swimming is rare, feel intimidated
- The copy should make someone think: "This is exactly how I feel. These people understand me."
- **High emotional shift** (fear → confidence) is the product's biggest conversion lever
- This is what makes SwimBuddz unique vs every other swim club: it's not about laps, it's about **transformation**

**How it maps to the homepage:**
- Directly validates Section 15 of the Implementation Guide (Copy Rewrite — Emotion-Led Messaging)
- The hero headline alternatives we proposed ("Scared of deep water? You're not alone." / "From fear to freestyle") are *exactly* what Rolf is recommending
- Testimonials need to show **transformation stories**, not generic praise
- The "Who SwimBuddz Is For" cards should speak to feelings, not just categories

## Secondary Insights

### Community Value Diminishes Over Time

> "My first two years [in YPO], every single time I spoke to a member, the value was unbelievable... I don't get the same thing anymore. It's probably sub 10% what it was to me."

**Implication for SwimBuddz:**
- The initial "I learned to swim" phase is high value. Once someone is comfortable, the community's value drops unless there's a **second act**
- This is exactly why the three-tier model matters: Community → Club → Academy gives people progression
- The volunteer system, Bubbles rewards, challenges, and events are all **retention mechanisms** that create value beyond the initial learning phase
- The business model can't rely solely on community membership — the **Academy (coaching/instruction)** is where the real business is

### Target Audience = Fearful Swimmers Who Can Pay

> "Focus on high emotive change... people who are in fear... who can't swim or operate in a high state of anxiety or fear around water."

Combined with the ability to pay, Rolf recommended:
- **Island (affluent) residents who are scared of water** — smaller group but can pay and have high emotional shift
- Not the mainland (large audience, high emotion, but lower ability to pay)
- Not already-capable swimmers on the island (can pay, but low emotional shift = low perceived value)

**Implication for website:**
- The website tone should resonate with someone who *can afford* ₦20,000/year but is *intimidated* by the idea of swimming
- This is an aspirational, fear-conquering narrative — similar to how Peloton markets to people who feel intimidated by gyms

### Seasonality Matters

> "Q3 (rainy season) = almost nobody comes. Q4 = most swimmers."

Rolf advised:
- Build a **seasonality calendar** mapping: temperature, rain, school holidays, cultural events (when people have cash pinches), religious periods
- Use it to **calibrate expectations** — a drop in engagement during rainy season isn't a problem, it's expected
- Don't let seasonal dips create emotional panic
- During low seasons, focus on **content, community maintenance, and planning** rather than growth pushes

**Implication for the site:**
- The homepage could benefit from **seasonal messaging** (e.g., "New year, new stroke" in January vs "Ember month is swim month" in October)
- The Sessions section should reflect seasonal activity — hide empty states during off-season

### SwimBuddz is the ONLY Swimming Community in Nigeria

> "If you go on ChatGPT, I ask for swimming communities in Lagos. Ours is the only community."

**Implication:**
- This is a powerful **trust signal** that should be on the homepage
- Consider adding: "Nigeria's first and only structured swimming community" or similar first-mover claim
- This is a moat — but only if the community is actively growing and visible

### Purpose-Driven = Free Labour (Volunteers)

> "Having a purpose like that, you'll find free labour, supporting your cause, because it's a purpose that matters."

**Implication:**
- The volunteer system isn't just operational efficiency — it's a feature of the purpose-driven model
- People WANT to volunteer for something meaningful
- The website should frame volunteering as **joining a movement**, not filling shifts
- This validates our recommendation to create a proper volunteer page with purpose-driven messaging

## One-Line Summary

**SwimBuddz's competitive advantage is emotional transformation (fear → confidence) in an underserved market (Nigeria has no other swim community), and the business model works when you align hours-in-pool with pool operator incentives — but the website currently doesn't speak to the fear that drives people to seek community in the first place.**
